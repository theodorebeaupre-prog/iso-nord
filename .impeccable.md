## Design Context

### Users
Premium clients across real estate, commercial brands, events, and agencies in Québec and beyond. They evaluate vendors visually — the website IS the portfolio pitch. They arrive skeptical and need to feel the craft before they read a word of copy.

### Brand Personality
Still. Tectonic. Precise.

ISO Nord is a full-production studio (photo + video + drone under one roof). The differentiator is not the gear — it's the cinematic eye behind certified TC piloting. The brand should feel like the title card of a Villeneuve film: commanding without shouting, slow without being empty.

### Aesthetic Direction
Nordic minimalist. Dark. Deliberate.
- Background near-black (#09090b), text near-white (#f5f5f5), lime accent (#c8ff00) used sparingly on hover only
- Font: Helvetica Neue weight 200 (ultra-light) — primary. Barlow Condensed weight 300 for small labels and numerals only (secondary contrast, not monoculture)
- Anti-references: no warm serif Squarespace photographer feel, no dark-orange action-sports drone bro aesthetic
- Gallery placeholders: subtle film grain texture (SVG feTurbulence, screen blend mode), suggesting analogue photography

### Design Principles
1. **Restraint as signal** — every empty centimeter is intentional. Clients who feel rushed feel cheap.
2. **Editorial not template** — services as a vertical numbered list with thin rules, not icon cards in a grid
3. **Accent earns its appearance** — #c8ff00 lime shows only on hover interaction, nowhere else
4. **Typography does the heavy lifting** — size and weight contrast replace decorative chrome
5. **Grain over gloss** — film-grain texture on placeholders; the site should feel analogue even before real images load
